## Overview

Developed as a research intern at the UNLV Drones & Autonomous Systems Lab, this project built an end-to-end pipeline for classifying EEG brainwave signals to control a prosthetic robotic arm. The work resulted in an IEEE conference publication in 2020.

## Research Question

Can consumer-grade EEG headsets (under $300) provide sufficient signal fidelity to reliably classify motor imagery signals for prosthetic control — without the expense of medical-grade equipment?

## Methodology

### Data Collection

- Used **OpenBCI Cyton** (8-channel, 250 Hz) to record EEG during motor imagery tasks
- Subjects imagined left-hand, right-hand, and rest states across 60-trial sessions
- Collected 1,200 labeled trials from 8 subjects

### Signal Processing

- Bandpass filtering: 8–30 Hz (mu + beta bands)
- Common Spatial Pattern (CSP) for spatial filtering
- Feature extraction: power spectral density per channel per band

### Classification

- Trained a **1D Convolutional Neural Network** (PyTorch) on windowed EEG segments
- Compared against SVM + LDA baselines
- CNN achieved **87.2% 3-class accuracy** vs 78.4% for best baseline

### Prosthetic Integration

- Inference ran on Raspberry Pi 4 with under 80ms latency
- Interfaced with a 6-DOF robotic arm via serial protocol
- Real-time demo: subjects controlling arm to pick up objects with ~85% success rate

## Publication

> "Motor Imagery Classification for Prosthetic Control Using Lightweight CNNs on Consumer EEG Hardware." *IEEE Young Professionals Conference Proceedings*, 2020.
